# 归还他者

## 1.研究问题

为什么人与人的误解会不断发生,即使是自己熟悉的人?

## 2.核心概念

**归还**: 承认人的思想,认知与人性是无法被完全剖析的
**他人画像**: 认知因为对于他人的接触形成对他人的认知模型,而非真实的他人

## 3.核心观点

认知在与他人的接触,见识了他人的言行举止,但是并未完全理解他人,而是在目前所接触他人的信息建立了一个认知模型,认知的模型并不是真正的他人,本文称之为**他人画像**
理论并不提倡完全去理解他人,而是仍然可以保留认知的模型,同时承认他人是无法被完全剖析的,即是本文的核心概念**归还**
而此理论同样适用于自身,自身认知对于自己的自我模型则更加深刻,难以**归还**

## 4.观点论证

人在理解他人时,必须依赖已有经验
随着接触增加,画像不断更新,但它仍然只是模型,而不是他人本身
当认知观察自己时,观察行为本身会影响观察结果,因此无法像观察他人一样保持距离
而不理解他人代价相对较小,而不理解自己带来的是自我怀疑,影响自我的尊严和自我认同
自我拥有自己最多的信息,认知容易产生"我完全知道我自己"的错觉,反而更难发现和承认自己在变化
因此相对于**归还**他人,对于自己更加难以做到

## 6.局限

理论仅解释现实中人认知现象,而不能解释理想状态下和抽象逻辑内的现象

## 7.澄清

虽然理论认为他人与自我无法被认知完全剖析,但是并不代表理论提倡完全不保留他人画像,而是在承认这种无法完全剖析性的同时,仍然可以保留部分他人画像

# Theory: Returning The Other

## 1. Research Question
Why do misunderstandings continuously occur between people, even among those who are deeply familiar with one another?

## 2. Core Concepts
* **Restitution (归还):** The act of acknowledging that human thought, cognition, and nature can never be fully dissected or completely understood.
* **The Avatar of the Other (他人画像):** A cognitive model formed through contact with another person, representing one's perception of them rather than who that person truly is.

## 3. Core Arguments
Through contact with others, our cognition observes their words and deeds. However, instead of fully understanding them, it constructs a cognitive model based on the information gathered so far. This model is not the actual person, but what this paper terms **"The Avatar of the Other."**

This theory does not advocate for an impossible, total comprehension of others. Instead, it suggests that we can retain these cognitive models while simultaneously acknowledging that others can never be fully dissected—a premise defined here as **"Restitution."**

Furthermore, this theory applies to the self. Because our cognitive model of ourselves is far deeper and more entrenched, achieving **Restitution** toward oneself is considerably more difficult.

## 4. Argumentation & Validation
* **Reliance on Experience:** To understand others, individuals must rely on their existing experiences.
* **The Model vs. Reality:** As interactions increase, the "Avatar" is continuously updated; yet, it remains merely a model, not the actual person.
* **The Observer Effect on Self:** When cognition observes itself, the act of observation alters the result. Consequently, one cannot maintain the same objective distance when looking inward as they do when observing others.
* **The Cost of Misunderstanding:** Failing to understand others carries a relatively low cost. In contrast, failing to understand oneself breeds self-doubt, directly harming one's dignity and self-identity.
* **The Illusion of Total Knowledge:** Because individuals possess the greatest amount of information about themselves, cognition easily falls into the illusion of "I know exactly who I am." This makes it significantly harder to detect and admit one's own internal changes.
* **Conclusion:** Therefore, compared to practicing **Restitution** toward others, doing so for oneself is far more difficult to achieve.

## 6. Limitations
This theory only explains cognitive phenomena within human reality; it does not apply to phenomena under ideal states or within abstract logical frameworks.

## 7. Clarification
Although the theory asserts that both others and the self cannot be fully dissected by cognition, it does not advocate for abandoning the "Avatar of the Other" entirely. Rather, it suggests that we can still retain partial aspects of these avatars while fully recognizing their inherent limitations and incompleteness.
